Rule 0: Common Sense and Courtesy

0.1) Act in good faith. The spirit of a rule supersedes its wording.

0.2) Do not spam, troll, grief, or otherwise intentionally ruin the game for others.

0.3) Do not harass other players. If someone expresses OOC discomfort, stop immediately and rethink what you are doing. Sexual harassment will not be tolerated.

  • Deadchat is not the place for toxicity. It is normal to be upset, but temper yourself. Do not flame other players over IC events.

0.4) Do not intentionally spark drama or arguments. Do your best to keep the peace.

0.5) Do not use slurs under any circumstance. Bigotry is strictly prohibited.

0.6) Do not share in-character events from an ongoing round through OOC means.

0.7) Cooperate with staff when ticketed. Do not lie or verbally abuse staff in a ticket. If a staff member tells you to do something in an administrative capacity, you must comply.

0.8) Respect the rulings of staff members. If you want to contest a ruling, file a ticket on the server Discord.

Rule 1: Immersion and Atmosphere

1.1) Do not break immersion for other players. Maintain the atmosphere of Fallout’s setting at all times.

  • Fortuna explicitly seeks to highlight the more serious elements of Fallout. “Wild wasteland” and other silly — but immersion-breaking — elements are generally not permitted. “Fallout is wacky” is not an excuse for disrupting the atmosphere,

1.2) Do not reference elements of modern culture in-character. Fallout canon diverges from real life around the 1950’s. Memes have no place in our setting.

  • Bruh”, “femboy”, “soyboy”, “beta cuck”, “based”, “sus”, “sneed”, “cope and seethe”, “LARP”, etc. are all examples of terms that must not be used in-character. This list is not exhaustive; use your own judgement.

1.3) Name your characters appropriately. Your character name must be suitable for the setting and atmosphere of Fallout. It is up to staff discretion whether or not a name is appropriate for the setting.

  • Your name may not be a reference to famous individuals, real or fictional.
  • Gag names or overly explicit names are not permitted.
  • Members of the Brotherhood of Steel and the NCR must have both a first and last name; no nicknames or titles.
  • Legionaries must have Romanized names.

1.4) Do not explicitly reference OOC mechanics such as respawning, AHELPing, admin-healing,  round timers, etc. When in doubt, use LOOC, or obscure your language such that it fits within the setting and does not hurt immersion.

  • Please do not refer to staff as “the gods” in-character. Use language appropriate for your role (i.e. “High Command” for NCR, “Mars” for Legion, “the Elders” for BoS, etc.)

1.5) Do not play combat-heavy roles if you are not confident in your ability to fight. A Veteran Ranger dying to radroaches is immersion-breaking.

1.6) No End-of Round Grief (EORG) outside of the train. Maintain immersion until the round ends.

Rule 2: Heavy Roleplay Standards

2.1) Fortuna is a roleplay-heavy server. Players must be fluent in English and capable of coherent communication.

  • Players are expected to use punctuation and proper grammar/spelling. Failure to meet this standard may result in administrative action.
  • Quality roleplay will be rewarded. If players present themselves to be skilled roleplayers, they may be trusted with special privileges.

2.2) Continuity and canon persists between rounds. You are expected to recall past relations and interactions. Events may only be decanonized if all players involved agree, or if staff deem it necessary.

  • Player character deaths are to be retconned as serious injuries. Avoid referencing the fact that you have died in-character; approach it as though you were merely unconscious, comatose, or otherwise incapacitated.
  • Players may opt to make their own character deaths permanent; it is their discretion. However, if a character voluntarily commits suicide, it will be considered a permanent character kill. This does not apply to Legionaries.
  • You may not escalate to lethal on player characters over events from prior rounds.

2.3) You must roleplay a consistent character and respond to in-character events like a real person would. Characters that are deemed unrealistic or unsuitable to Fallout’s setting may be subject to administrative action.

  • No neon hair. Hair dye exists, but keep it tasteful.
  • Reasonable height; anyone taller than 7 feet or shorter than 4 feet should have significant deformities and must be roleplayed accordingly.
  • Reasonable appearance; you may not be a half-deathclaw mutant or a furred anthropomorphic creature. If you would like special privileges to play a non-standard character, ask server staff.

2.4) You are NOT the protagonist. You may not play a Mary Sue. Your character must have strengths and weaknesses, and these should be demonstrated through your roleplay.

2.5) Metaknowledge is prohibited; your character may only possess knowledge that they would reasonably attain, either through their background or through in-character events. There is a separation between what you as a player know, and what your character knows.

  • Do not communicate with other players out-of-character to gain in-character advantages — this is known as metacommunications.
  • Always keep in mind that your character (in most cases) was born in the 23rd century, with little to no knowledge of pre-war America. The only exceptions to this are pre-war ghouls, and to a lesser degree Followers of the Apocalypse and the Brotherhood of Steel.
  • Characters who are related (i.e. siblings, parent/child, etc.) may not share info off-screen. This is still considered metagaming.

2.6) Do not hop between roles or factions without in-character progression and strong reasoning.

Rule 3: Mechanics

3.1) In all cases, roleplay supersedes mechanics. Do not prioritize mechanics over roleplay; treat them as a supplement to RP.

  • If an otherwise reasonable action cannot be represented mechanically, players are allowed and encouraged to represent that action through their roleplay. Other players are expected to respect this.
  • That said, no godmodding/powerplaying. This is taking control of another player's character during roleplay, and is generally not permissible.

3.2) Do not powergame. Powergaming is defined as abusing game mechanics in a way that is damaging to roleplay, especially in a way that is unrealistic for a character to be capable of.

  • Exploiting dungeons to complete them in an unintended way through abuse of mechanics is considered powergaming.
  • Some other examples of powergaming, generally speaking:
  • A BoS Paladin mixing complex medicines
  • A Follower Doctor solo-wiping a faction
  • A Legionary with no medical training performing surgery.

3.3) Do not utilize exploits, do not share exploits with other players. Report them privately to staff or our development team.

3.4) Do not mechanically engage with players who are SSD, AFK, or otherwise unresponsive but alive.

  • Do not abuse this protection. Intentionally disconnecting to avoid consequences for one’s actions will result in administrative action.
  • If you must disconnect for the entire round, please despawn yourself at a Matrix point.

3.5) You may not behead someone without at least one emote describing the action, as well as ample in-character reasoning.

  • Beheading for the sole purpose of preventing revival is prohibited.
  • Hiding bodies or heads to prevent revival is seen as poor conduct. Burial or burning corpses is acceptable; hiding them in a closet or behind a locked door is not.

3.6) Off-duty roles must not act as regular on-duty faction members. Off-duty personnel must defer authority to those on-duty. Do not pull rank when off-duty.

  • If a faction is being attacked, off-duties MAY NOT defend their base. They are required to matrix. Only engage in faction combat if it is self-defense.
  • Off-duty BoS may not leave the bunker.

3.7) Faction members must wear faction armor in combat. They may not discard their faction armor in favor of unaffiliated or generic armor. Painting generic armor in faction colors does not quality it as faction armor.

Rule 4: Escalation and Combat

In order to escalate properly, follow the steps indicated in this chart.

4.1) In nearly all circumstances, combat MUST have ample escalation and in-character justification. Do not escalate on another player without strong IC reasoning.

  • NEVER INITIATE COMBAT WITHOUT ACTIVATING YOUR COMBAT INDICATOR. The default button to toggle this is C.
  • Follow the steps indicated in the Escalation Flowchart.
  • If you provide conditions to someone while escalating (i.e. "If you pull a gun, you're dead!") and they actively take an action that violates those conditions, you are free to attack them.

4.2) Do not use bolas without escalating. Never throw a bola without activating combat mode. Un-escalated bolas will be punished as harshly as any other form of poorly-escalated combat.

4.3) Do not handcuff others without escalating, either via verbal warning or an emote that clearly presents your intention to handcuff them.

  • If a person is unconscious or involved in an ongoing fight, this is not necessary.

4.4) If a player flees from active escalation, they may be attacked immediately.

  • If a player attempts to handcuff, strip, disarm or stun another without escalation, they may be attacked immediately.
  • Dragging away bodies, healing combatants, or otherwise aiding members of an active fight is accepting escalation.

4.5) If more than two minutes pass between combat, players are required to re-escalate.

4.6) Faction members may join fights involving other faction members if and only if both participants are wearing their faction uniform, with their tags (if applicable) clearly visible.

  • NCR and Legion should remain in uniform unless given explicit approval for a covert infiltration (via raid approval), in which case they may wear each others' gear explicitly for that purpose.
  • Non-faction members must make their intentions and allegiance clear before joining combat, faction v. faction or otherwise.

4.7) Base raids, faction or player, require explicit permission from online staff. If you wish to raid a base you must AHELP for permission.

  • Base raids require clear in-character justification. Staff may deny raids if they are unjustified or if the balance of power is grossly disproportionate.
  • Refer to the Guide to Raiding for a more comprehensive description of server rules and standards regarding base raids.

4.8) Do not escalate on or steal from other players without provocation until at least 30 minutes into the round.

Rule 5: Mature Content

5.1) This is an 18+ community. Underage users will be swiftly removed, without exception.

  • The staff team reserves the right to perform non-invasive age-checks on any member of the community.
  • All characters that appear in-game must be over 18. Underage characters are not permitted under any circumstance.

5.2) Mature content is permitted. This includes erotic roleplay. Explicit consent is required before engaging in sexual content, either in-character, in OOC notes, or in LOOC. Consent may be withdrawn at any point.

  • It is expected that players treat ERP as an extension of regular roleplay, rather than a primary focus. Hypersexual characters may be moderated.
  • Do not harass other players for engaging in ERP. If you cannot tolerate mature content, leave.

5.3) Non-consensual Erotic Roleplay is forbidden. No non-consensual ERP may take place between any player characters. Given the nature of the setting, sexual assault and similar content still exists within the universe and it may still be used as part of a character's backstory, but it should never take place on our server or be canonized between player characters.

5.4) Extreme content is not permitted. This includes (but is not limited to) any depiction of sexual gore, scatophilia, pedophilia, vorarephilia, or bestiality. Keep it off our server.

  • Players may use LOOC to opt out of any objectionable content, within reason. Please be respectful of others and their sensitivities.

5.4) Do not include oversexualized descriptions in flavortexts. Any explicit reference art must be clearly marked as NSFW.
